# Presentation

This repository contains the write-ups and the solutions that I came up with to the various challenges of Pico CTF, a CTF compeition arranged every year by the Carnegie Mellon University.

When I first started working on those challenges, I was trying to reach the answer as fast as possible. And I was basing my success at these challenges on how fast I could solve them. This was not working. I found myself to be quite frustrated and feeling quite bad whenever I was getting stuck of submitting a wrong answer. 

I started doing those challenges with only minimal skills in the various topics of the CTF. 

So, after a while, I changed my approach. I started looking at those various challenges no longer as a competition, but rather as a learning tool.

I started going much slower when going through the challenges. The goal was now to slowly but surely come to a full understanding of the challenge before moving on to the next one. My approach would not be very exploratory in nature, acquiring knowledge as I went about solving the challenges and trying to wrap my head around each challenges as much as possible. Even if that meant working on the challenge even after catching the flag.

Even with that mindset, solving challenges would take me days. Countless of hours. Countless trials and errors. Spendinng a lot of brain power on trying to figure things out. Slowly, through consistent effort, the fog began to clear. Even for intermediate challenges, the amount of solves sit at around 1000. This is very low, especially when considering that "easy" challenges easily have over 40 000 solves.

And, even with all those hours and efforts put into figuring things out, I would still heavily rely on external documentation, on the web and not least on ChatGPT. I used a lot of external help. Even at times receiving direct suggestions on how to capture the flag and on which vulnerability to exploit. But then I would need to reasearch the vulnerability and on how to execute. So even receiving a solution from ChatGPT involved a lot of mental effort to actually end up capturing the flag.

So, understanding how difficult it was and how much these challenges are a basis on which to acquire knowledge and learn new skills, I enjoyed the process much more. It did not make me go faster, it did not make me any better, but it made me more resilient and helped me in not giving up.

So, after going through all those efforts to come with thorough solutions, installing the necessary environments, learning the various vulnerabilities, learning the various tools of the trade, I thought that I could not let all those efforts be forgotten. So I thought that investing in producing thorough write-ups would be worth it. So I created this repository. Just like I have a repository about solving the challenges of Advent of code 2024.

All of those write-ups are fully released in the public domain. I am really proud of the open source culture in the IT world. This allows the field to evolve very fast. And, ultimately, this is a huge benefit to humanity.

However, I am not reproducing the challenge description and inserting the challenge materials in this repository.
